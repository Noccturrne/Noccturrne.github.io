History of Video Games

Video games are digital games that can be played on a computer device (e.g., tablets, smartphones, laptops, consoles, etc.). Originally designed simply for entertainment, video games have grown into their own industry and field of learning, generating billions of dollars each year.

The Beginning (1950s)

  • The first computerized Video Game made was tic-tac-toe called Bertie the brain in 1950
  • October 1958, A Physicist named William Higinbotham Makes a tennis video game similar to the video game Pong
  • Another game with physics called SpaceWar! Is made in about 1959-1962

Video Games at Home (1960-1983)

  • In 1966 a man named Ralph Baer with a few friends made “the Brown Box” and was a prototype for the very first multiplayer and first multiprogram video game. The company he was working for, Sanders Associates Inc. licensed the system to Magnavox
  • In 1972 Magnavox Releases the design as the Magnavox Odyssey
  • 1975 Atari’s game system is made. Its first game being PONG
